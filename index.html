About Me

Born in Aleppo Syria, raised in Toronto Canada, Aleppo, and Saudi Arabia. Ahmad comes from a long line of teachers, scholars, and writers, including his grandparents, and great-grandparents. His mother has been teaching the book of Allah for over 35 years, and his father is a scholar in the Islamic tradition.


Although his Dawah and career in the automotive industry seem admirable to others, to Ahmad, it feels quaint at best. “Each and every one of us has a destiny to stand before the Lord of the Worlds, and we will be taken to account for the time we spent on this Earth.” This is something Bostani takes very seriously. Working with scholars on their own projects has inspired Ahmad to take on projects of his own. This is when he decided to launch his Instagram page; @abostani, participate in religious debates over live streams, and public speaking and write his own texts, The first of which, is “The Seekers Etiquette”. “I chose to write this book because as many scholars and academics would testify, many people come to knowledge today as a means of argumentation or even a tool for life, not a destination. Allah SWT says, ‘Read in the name of your Lord who created…’ This is indicative that our learnings and understandings should be for the sake of God, but how would one do that with no etiquette?”


Furthermore, Bostani is part of several Islamic organizations internationally, aiding in raising funds for charity, Islamic festivals, Conferences, and more.
